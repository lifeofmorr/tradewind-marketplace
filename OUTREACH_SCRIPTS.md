brackets, but keep the core value props consistent: **AI listing generator**,
**lead routing**, **featured placement**, **analytics**, and the
**60-day free launch offer**.

Replace `{{founder_name}}` with **Don Morrison**, `{{founder_phone}}`
with your number, and `{{calendar_link}}` with your scheduling URL.

## 2. Boat dealer cold call script (with objection handling)

**Opener (30 seconds):**
"Hey {{contact_name}}, this is {{founder_name}} with TradeWind — we're a
new marketplace for boats and high-end autos that just launched in the
Southeast. I'll keep this quick: do you have 60 seconds, or should I
catch you better tomorrow?"

**If yes — pitch (90 seconds):**
"Real fast — we're handing the first 30 dealers a 60-day free trial,
no card, no commitment. What you get is your full inventory live with
an AI listing generator that writes the descriptions for you, lead
routing that shows you each buyer's financing pre-qual, and featured
placement for the first month so your boats sit above everyone else's.
We're not asking for exclusivity. You can keep YachtWorld and BoatTrader.
We just want our marketplace to be the best place buyers come to research
{{specific_category}} in {{state}} — and you've got the inventory we
want our buyers to see."

**Ask:**
"Could I send you a 90-second loom of the dealer dashboard, and book 15
minutes for a real demo?"

**Objections:**

> "We already have YachtWorld and BoatTrader."

"Good — keep them. We're additive, not exclusive. The pitch is: your
inventory is already photographed and described. Importing to TradeWind
is a CSV upload and 10 minutes of my time, and you get 60 free days to
see if our buyers convert better. Worst case, you walk away with a free
AI-generated listing kit you can reuse on your existing channels."

> "I don't have time to learn a new platform."

"Totally fair. That's why for the first 30 dealers I do the import myself.
You email me your CSV or your YachtWorld export, I bring up your inventory
on TradeWind, and the only thing you do is approve the photos. That's it.
Time investment is 10 minutes."

> "How much does it cost after 60 days?"

"Starter is $99/month for 10 listings, Pro is $299/month for 50, Premier
is $799/month for unlimited. Your tier locks for 12 months, so if you
sign in the first 30 days you're paying the launch rate even when public
pricing goes up. And there's no contract — you can cancel anywhere from
day 1 through day 60 and never see a charge."

> "I'll think about it."

"Of course. Can I send you the loom and just text-follow up Friday? No
hard ask, just want to make sure you've actually seen the product
before you decide."

> "I tried something like this before and it didn't work."

"What was the something? [Listen.] OK so here's the difference for us:
{{specific_differentiation}}. And for 60 days you don't pay a dollar to
find out, so the worst case is you spent 10 minutes on a CSV. Worth it?"

**Close (always book a calendar slot before hanging up):**
"Cool — let me grab a slot on your calendar before we hang up. Tuesday
2pm or Thursday 10am work better?"

## 6. Private seller outreach (Facebook Marketplace, Craigslist)

> Use this **only** for sellers who already have a public listing on
> a major site. Personal DM, never bulk. One sentence about why you
> think their listing is good before pitching.

**Template:**

"Hey {{name}}, saw your {{year}} {{make}} {{model}} listing — beautiful
boat/car. I run TradeWind, a new marketplace for boats and autos in the
Southeast. We've been getting better-quality buyer traffic than Facebook
or Craigslist (way fewer time-wasters), and listings are free during
launch. If I cross-post yours for you (10 minutes of work on my end,
zero on yours) and one of our buyers ends up making the deal, would you
mind if we featured the listing in our launch email? No pressure, no
obligation. Just trying to fill the marketplace with quality stuff."

**Why it works:**
- Names a specific item — not template spam
- Removes friction — "10 minutes of my time, zero on yours"
- Asks for a tiny commitment — feature in email, not a contract
- Honest about the launch context — they don't feel marketed to

## 11. Tone & content guardrails

- **Always name the dealership/broker by name.** No "Hi there".
- **Reference at least one specific piece of their inventory** in
  the first sentence of any email or DM.
- **Never overpromise on traffic.** Cite real numbers from the metrics
  sheet only.
- **Always offer to do the import yourself.** Removes the biggest
  objection in the first 30 days.
- **Always close with a calendar link or a specific time.** "Let me
  know" gets ignored. "Thursday 10am or Friday 2pm?" gets a reply.
- **Never disparage YachtWorld, BoatTrader, AutoTrader.** Position as
  additive, not replacement.
- **End every Day 14 follow-up with the "one word reply" ask.** It's
  the single highest-yield opt-out we've found in early testing.

## 12. Reply triage rubric

| Reply contains | Action |
|---|---|
| "interested", "tell me more", "demo" | Book within 24h |
| Specific objection ("price", "time", "tool") | Reply same day with one-line answer + calendar link |
| "Send the loom" / "send a deck" | Send within 4 hours, follow up Day 3 |
| "Pass" / "not interested" | Mark as `lost`, remove from list, send a one-line "thanks for letting me know — door's open if it changes" |
| Auto-reply / OOO | Wait until OOO end + 1 day, resend Day 1 email |
| Bounce | Mark as `bounced`, look for second email or LinkedIn |
| No reply after Day 14 | Mark as `cold`, queue for a 60-day re-touch |

This is the playbook. Run it daily.
