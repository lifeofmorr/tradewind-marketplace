# TradeWind Demo Call Guide

**Owner:** Don Morrison
**Date:** 2026-05-27
**Format:** 10-minute live screen-share on `https://tradewind-marketplace.vercel.app`
**Tone:** Founder-led, premium calm. Show the product; let it carry the weight.

---

## Universal flow (first 2 minutes, every call)

**0:00 – 0:30 — Greet and frame**

> "Thanks for the time. I'm Don — I'm the founder, not a sales person. I'd
> rather spend 10 minutes on the parts that actually matter for your side
> of the business than walk through a slide deck."

**0:30 – 1:00 — Quick context**

> "TradeWind is a premium marketplace and transaction platform for boats,
> autos, and aircraft. It's live beta — everything you're about to see is
> real software. Some inventory is curated for demo; real listings from
> seed partners are coming in."

**Show:** `/beta` page. Point to the vertical toggle and search.

**1:00 – 2:00 — Role pivot**

Ask: *"Before I drive, tell me — what does a typical week look like for you
in terms of listings, buyer contacts, and where things break down?"*

Listen for 60 seconds. Then go to their vertical.

## Role-specific branches

---

### Yacht / Boat Broker (minutes 2–8)

**What to show:**

1. `/boats` — browse listings, filters (hull type, length, engine hours).
2. One listing detail — point to Asset Passport (condition history,
   maintenance records), Deal Score, True Cost estimate.
3. Buyer request flow — show the inquiry or pre-buy request panel.
4. Transaction Room — "when a buyer gets serious, this opens automatically."

**Questions to ask:**

- "How many listings do you typically have active at once?"
- "Where do your qualified buyers come from today — referrals, MLS, something else?"
- "What's the biggest friction point between a serious inquiry and actually closing?"

**What not to promise:**

- Real buyer traffic is not guaranteed in beta.
- MLS integration is roadmap, not shipped.
- Don't promise specific listing volumes from your network.

**Close:**

> "What would make the Asset Passport or the deal room actually useful for
> your side of a transaction?"

---

### Aircraft Broker (minutes 2–8)

**What to show:**

1. `/aircraft` — browse listings, aircraft category filters (piston, turbine,
   jet, helicopter).
2. One listing detail — specs panel (total time, avionics, annual status),
   Asset Passport for airframe and engine logs, pre-buy inspection request.
3. Aviation services partner panel — airframe shops, avionics, ferry pilots.
4. Transaction Room — show how pre-buy coordination and offer flow work together.

**Questions to ask:**

- "Are you doing pre-buys on most deals, or just the larger ones?"
- "What tools are you using today to manage the documentation side of a transaction?"
- "Where do qualified buyers find you right now?"

**What not to promise:**

- FAA records integration is credential-ready but not live in beta.
- Don't promise specific aircraft inventory from your network.
- No AD compliance tracking in current beta.

**Close:**

> "What would make this actually useful for an aircraft transaction — the
> pre-buy flow, the documentation, or something else?"

---

### Exotic / Classic Dealer (minutes 2–8)

**What to show:**

1. `/autos` — browse inventory, filters (make, year, condition, price).
2. One listing detail — Deal Score (market comparison), Asset Passport
   (service history, ownership records), True Cost estimate.
3. Side-by-side comparison view — show how buyers evaluate two listings.
4. Dealer dashboard preview — listing management, buyer activity, inquiry threads.

**Questions to ask:**

- "How are buyers currently finding your inventory — your site, social, aggregators?"
- "Do you get a lot of tire-kickers, or are your inquiries generally qualified?"
- "What part of the sales process costs you the most time right now?"

**What not to promise:**

- AI listing assistant is in beta — accuracy improves with real data.
- Carfax/AutoCheck integration is credential-ready, not surfaced publicly yet.
- Don't promise specific buyer volume.

**Close:**

> "If you listed two or three vehicles here, what would you need to see
> from the buyer side before you called it worth the effort?"

---

### Service Provider (minutes 2–8)

**What to show:**

1. Service provider profile — how they appear in search, partner matching.
2. Lead request flow — buyer or dealer submits a service request, provider
   receives a matched lead with context (asset type, location, urgency).
3. Request thread — show how communication and coordination happen in-platform.
4. Partner network page — how service providers appear alongside listings
   in the relevant vertical.

**Questions to ask:**

- "Where does most of your work come from today — referrals, repeat clients,
  aggregators?"
- "How many inspection or service requests do you typically handle per month?"
- "Is visibility your problem, or is it lead quality — or both?"

**What not to promise:**

- Guaranteed lead volume is not promised in beta.
- Payment processing for service jobs is roadmap, not live.
- Don't promise specific partner categories that aren't live in their area.

**Close:**

> "If the lead flow here matched what you get from your best referral
> source, what would that be worth to you monthly?"

---

### Lender / Insurance / Escrow (minutes 2–8)

**What to show:**

1. Partner network page — how financial and insurance partners appear in
   the platform alongside listings.
2. Request capture — when a buyer or dealer flags financing or insurance
   interest on a listing, the partner receives the lead with deal context.
3. Transaction Room — show how lender and escrow participants are added
   to a deal thread.
4. API/integration readiness — mention Plaid (credential-ready), escrow
   partner API (credential-ready), VIN/hull verification.

**Questions to ask:**

- "How do you typically get connected to a deal today — broker referral,
  buyer direct, something else?"
- "Do you have an API or preferred data format for deal intake?"
- "What information would you need from the asset side before you could
  quote or approve?"

**What not to promise:**

- Live transaction volume is not guaranteed in beta.
- Don't promise exclusivity in their category or geography.
- Compliance/licensing review for your state is not handled in-platform.

**Close:**

> "If we got deal intake flowing to you with asset-level context already
> attached, what would that change about how you process requests?"

## After the 8-minute branch

**8:00 – 9:00 — Surface what they care about**

> "You've seen the part that matters most for your business. What's the
> most useful thing here, and what's the thing you'd change first?"

Listen. Don't fill the silence.

**9:00 – 10:00 — Close**

> "I'm not opening this wide. I'm looking for a small group of people who
> will tell me what's actually wrong and what's missing. Beta partners get
> direct founder access, free through launch, and a real say in what we
> build next.
>
> What would make this actually useful for your side of the business?"

If they're interested: direct to `/beta` to fill out the form, or book a
follow-up call via the calendar link on `/beta`.

If they're not ready: "Completely fair. Is it worth me checking back in a
few months, or would you rather I leave it here?"

---

## What never to promise (any role)

- Real buyer traffic guarantees.
- Live integration with any third-party system not yet surfaced on `/beta`.
- Specific competitor displacement ("we replace X").
- Pricing that isn't published.
- Features that aren't live in the beta you just showed them.
