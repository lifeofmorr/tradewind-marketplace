# TradeWind 10-Minute Demo Script

**Owner:** Don Morrison
**Date:** 2026-05-27
**Format:** Live screen-share on `https://tradewind-marketplace.vercel.app/beta`.
**Tone:** Founder-led, premium-calm, never breathless.

The whole point of this demo is to be useful in 10 minutes. Not 25, not 45. The
prospect should leave with a clear picture of one or two things TradeWind does
that nothing else in their stack does today — and a real ask at the end.

---

## Before the call (3-minute prep)

- Open `/beta` in one tab and the prospect's company website in another.
- Skim the lead's notes in `/admin/outreach` — verticals, pain point,
  personalization angle.
- Have an example listing pulled up in their vertical (boat, auto, or
  aircraft). Don't pick the most expensive — pick the most realistic.
- Mute notifications. Share window not full-screen.
- Open `BETA_ONBOARDING_CHECKLIST.md` in a side tab in case they say yes
  to listing.

---

## Minute-by-minute

### 0:00 – 1:00 — Founder intro

**What to say:**

> "Thanks for the time. I'm Don — I'm building TradeWind. Quick context: I'm
> not a sales person, I'm the founder, and this is a small private beta. I'd
> rather use 10 minutes to show you the parts that might matter for
> [COMPANY] than walk through a generic deck."

**What to show:** TradeWind homepage at `/beta`.

**What NOT to promise:** No "we have thousands of users", no "we just raised."
Don't invoke traction.

---

### 1:00 – 2:00 — Homepage + beta context

**What to say:**

> "This is the beta. It's live, it's not a mockup — what you're seeing is
> real software. Some of the inventory is curated for demo, some is real
> from seed partners. We're inviting a small group of dealers, brokers,
> and service providers to list real inventory."

**What to show:**

- The hero / search.
- The vertical toggle (boats, autos, aircraft).
- Briefly point out the trust signals (Asset Passport, Deal Score,
  Verified badges).

**What NOT to promise:** Don't claim transaction volume that doesn't exist.

---

### 2:00 – 4:00 — Browse boats / autos / aircraft

**What to say:**

> "Buyers can search across boats, cars, and aircraft from one place,
> compare, save, and request more info. Filters are spec-aware — for
> aircraft we're indexing airframe and engine times; for boats it's hull,
> engine hours, location; for autos it's mileage, condition tier, and
> provenance."

**What to show:**

- Browse view for the prospect's vertical first.
- Use filters that match their typical inventory.
- Pull up a listing card and hover the badges.

**Talking points to weave in:**

- "Cross-vertical search is one of the few places we're meaningfully
  different from a vertical classifieds site."
- "Service provider listings live alongside inventory — surveyors,
  mechanics, transport, escrow."

---

### 4:00 – 6:00 — Listing detail (Asset Passport, Deal Score, True Cost, Pre-Buy)

**What to say:**

> "This is the listing detail page. The four things to look at are the
> Asset Passport, the Deal Score, the True Cost, and the Pre-Buy
> workflow. These are the parts buyers tell us they care about."

**What to show, in this order:**

1. **Asset Passport** — verified history, documents, inspections.
   "Everything a buyer would normally have to chase down — title,
   service records, surveys — lives in one place tied to the listing."
2. **Deal Score** — apples-to-apples comparison.
   "We score the deal against comparable inventory so buyers can
   tell what's actually a good price."
3. **True Cost** — beyond the sticker.
   "Insurance, financing, transport, slip / hangar / storage,
   estimated annual costs."
4. **Pre-Buy / Pre-Purchase Inspection** — connected workflow.
   "Buyer can request a pre-buy from a verified service provider
   without leaving the listing."

**What NOT to promise:** Don't promise that every listing has a full
Asset Passport yet — many are being filled in. Frame it as the model.

---

### 6:00 – 8:00 — Role-specific view

This is the most important two minutes of the demo. Adapt to the prospect.

#### If they're a Dealer / Yacht Broker / Auto Dealer

**What to show:**

- The dealer dashboard: inventory, listing status, lead flow.
- Create or open one listing — the listing builder form.
- A buyer request thread showing how leads land.

**What to say:**

> "Dealer dashboard. Inventory on the left, leads in the middle,
> Transaction Rooms on the right. Listing builder is one form,
> not seventeen. When a buyer wants more, you get a request in
> the same place — not a phone tag chain."

#### If they're an Aircraft Broker

**What to show:**

- Aircraft listing builder — airframe / engine / prop, equipment, logs.
- Pre-buy workflow tied to a Transaction Room.
- Buyer request flow.

**What to say:**

> "Aircraft side has all the fields you'd expect — airframe and engine
> times, prop, equipment, log status. Pre-buy is part of the deal
> thread, not a separate workflow. Title / escrow are partner-integrated
> on the back end."

#### If they're a Service Provider (surveyor, mechanic, transporter)

**What to show:**

- Service provider profile page (categories, coverage, credentials).
- Inbound request thread inside a Transaction Room.
- Quote workflow.

**What to say:**

> "Service providers sit inside the deal, not on a separate directory.
> When a buyer requests a pre-buy, a survey, or transport, you get a
> lead with the asset context already attached. You quote in-thread."

#### If they're a Buyer / Advisor

**What to show:**

- Compare view across two listings side-by-side.
- Saved listings.
- Feedback widget.

**What to say:**

> "Buyer-side is built around compare and save. We surface Deal Score,
> True Cost, and Asset Passport so buyers don't have to chase the same
> data across five sites."

---

### 8:00 – 9:00 — Transaction Room + trust

**What to say:**

> "This is the part of TradeWind that nothing else does. Once a buyer
> and seller are in a deal, the Transaction Room is the single thread:
> messaging, paperwork, pre-buy, financing, insurance, escrow, title.
> Partner integrations slot in here. The seller doesn't have to leave
> the platform to close."

**What to show:**

- Open a Transaction Room.
- Walk through the slots: pre-buy, financing, insurance, escrow,
  title, transport.
- Show the partner integration hooks (credential-ready, partner
  workflows wired in).

**What NOT to promise:** Don't promise every partner is integrated
*today*. Frame as: "partner-integrated by design — Plaid, VIN, and
the major escrow/title APIs are credential-ready and wiring in
order of partner demand."

---

### 9:00 – 10:00 — Feedback ask + beta interest

**What to say:**

> "That's the 10-minute tour. Two things I want to ask:
>
> One — what would make this actually useful for your side of the
> business? I'd rather hear the awkward answer than the polite one.
>
> Two — would [COMPANY] be open to listing one or two pieces of
> inventory in the beta? First 10 listings are free, no card. You'd
> be in a small group helping us shape what the dealer side looks
> like before we open it more broadly."

**What to capture:**

- What they liked (one specific feature).
- What's missing or confusing.
- Whether they'll list / partner / pay / pass.
- Any names of people they'd refer.

**Close line:**

> "What would make this actually useful for your side of the business?"

That's the question. Let them answer it. Don't fill the silence.

---

## What NOT to promise (master list)

- Volume / traction numbers that don't exist.
- "Coming soon" features — only "on the roadmap" or "shipped".
- Specific dates ("by Q3") unless you're 100% certain.
- Specific partner names that haven't signed an integration.
- Pricing below the standard beta terms (60-day free, first 10 listings).
- Custom features for their business — note it as feedback, don't commit.

---

## What feedback to capture

Open `/admin/outreach`, find the lead, and into the beta_pipeline row's
`feedback_notes` field, capture:

- The single feature that resonated most.
- The single feature that confused them.
- Their typical deal volume + average price (if shared).
- Where they currently list / source.
- Pricing reaction (positive, neutral, negative).
- Whether they'd refer a peer.

If they want to list real inventory, set `real_listing_candidate = true`
and move them to `stage = beta_invited`. See
`BETA_ONBOARDING_CHECKLIST.md`.

## After the call

Within 2 hours, send the post-demo follow-up (`POST_DEMO_FOLLOW_UPS.md`,
template 1). Same day, update the beta pipeline. Within 24 hours, send
the beta invite if they're a real listing candidate.

The demo is the conversion event. Don't let it cool.
