## Card 1 — Yacht / Boat Broker Demo

### Before the call

- [ ] Open `/boats` in one tab.
- [ ] Pull up one realistic listing (mid-range, not the most expensive) and
  have the listing detail page ready.
- [ ] Know roughly how many boat listings are currently live in the demo set.

### What to show (in order)

1. `/boats` — browse view, filters, pricing range.
2. Listing detail — Asset Passport panel (condition, maintenance, history),
   Deal Score badge, True Cost estimate.
3. Buyer request panel — how an inquiry or pre-buy request surfaces.
4. Transaction Room — "when a buyer gets serious, this opens automatically
   for both sides."

### Questions to ask

- "How many listings do you typically have active at once?"
- "Where do your qualified buyers come from today?"
- "What's the friction point between a serious inquiry and closing?"

### Do not promise

- Real buyer traffic is not guaranteed in beta.
- MLS or yacht brokerage association integration is roadmap, not shipped.
- Do not promise specific listing volumes from your network.

### Close

> "What would make the Asset Passport or the deal room actually useful for
> your side of a transaction?"

## Card 2 — Aircraft Broker Demo

### Before the call

- [ ] Open `/aircraft` in one tab.
- [ ] Pull up one aircraft listing — ideally a piston single or light twin,
  something realistic for most buyers.
- [ ] Have the aviation services partner section ready to show.

### What to show (in order)

1. `/aircraft` — browse view, category filters (piston, turbine, jet, helicopter).
2. Listing detail — specs panel (total time, SMOH, avionics, annual status),
   Asset Passport (airframe and engine logs, maintenance history).
3. Pre-buy inspection request — how a buyer or broker initiates a pre-buy
   from the listing.
4. Aviation services panel — airframe shops, avionics, ferry pilots matched
   to the listing's location and type.
5. Transaction Room — how pre-buy coordination and offer flow work together.

### Questions to ask

- "Are you doing pre-buys on most deals, or just the larger aircraft?"
- "What tools are you using today to manage the documentation side of
  a transaction?"
- "Where do qualified buyers find you right now?"

### Do not promise

- FAA records integration is credential-ready but not live in beta.
- No AD compliance tracking or airworthiness directive lookup in current beta.
- Do not promise specific aircraft inventory from your network.

### Close

> "What would make this actually useful for an aircraft transaction — the
> pre-buy flow, the documentation, or something else?"

## Card 3 — Service Provider Demo

### Before the call

- [ ] Know what type of service provider they are (marine shop, aviation
  maintenance, inspection, transport, detailing, etc.).
- [ ] Have the service provider profile view ready.
- [ ] Know which vertical their work falls in — boats, aircraft, or autos.

### What to show (in order)

1. Service provider profile — how they appear in partner search, what
   information is displayed to buyers and dealers.
2. Lead request flow — a buyer or dealer submits a service request from a
   listing, the provider gets a matched lead with context (asset type,
   location, urgency, contact info).
3. Request thread — in-platform communication and job coordination once
   the lead is accepted.
4. Partner network page — how service providers appear alongside listings
   in the relevant vertical (e.g., a marine surveyor appears next to
   boat listings in the buyer's area).

### Questions to ask

- "Where does most of your work come from today — referrals, repeat
  clients, aggregators?"
- "How many inspection or service requests do you handle per month?"
- "Is your problem visibility, lead quality, or both?"

### Do not promise

- Guaranteed lead volume at any level.
- Payment processing for service jobs — roadmap, not live.
- Exclusive territory or category placement in the partner network.

### Close

> "If the lead flow here matched what you get from your best referral
> source, what would that be worth to you monthly?"
