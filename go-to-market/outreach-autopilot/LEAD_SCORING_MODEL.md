# Lead Scoring Model — 1 to 5

Every lead in the CRM gets a score from 1 (skip) to 5 (top priority, contact today). The score is set during research and updated as we learn more.

---

## The 5-point rubric

### Score 5 — Top priority. Contact today.
Hit **all** of these:
- Premium or distinctive inventory / service (high-end, niche, well-presented)
- Active digital presence (recent listings, recent IG posts, working website)
- Decision-maker is identifiable and reachable (owner, principal, GM)
- High likely benefit from TradeWind (clear gap in their current presentation, lead flow, or workflow)
- Likely to provide candid feedback (independent operator, founder, owner-operator)
- Potential to either list real inventory **or** become a service/lender/insurance partner

### Score 4 — Strong. Contact this week.
Hit 4 of the 6 score-5 criteria. Usually: great fit but harder to reach, or great inventory but low social presence, or great person but small operation.

### Score 3 — Worth contacting. Contact this month.
- Mid-quality fit. Inventory exists but is generic.
- Likely will not be a flagship beta but could still convert.
- Or: a service provider in a vertical we have not yet seeded.

### Score 2 — Maybe later. Park.
- Big-box dealer with corporate gatekeeping
- Personal-data unclear, contact path foggy
- Inventory exists but the operator clearly doesn't run digital themselves

Hold in CRM; do not contact until we have a referral path.

### Score 1 — Skip / DNC.
- Out of category (e.g., truck-only dealer if we're not active there yet)
- Brand contact form only, no human reachable
- Bad public reputation (multiple complaints, fraud allegations)
- Competitor employee — never cold-pitch a competitor's staff

Mark Do Not Contact = Yes and remove from queue.

---

## Vertical-by-vertical priority

We always work the highest-leverage segments first. Within a given day's queue, weight contacts in this order:

### Top tier (start here)
1. **Independent yacht brokers** — willing to try new tools, often founder-run, premium inventory
2. **Small/mid boat dealers** (1–3 locations) — feel the pain of YachtWorld/BoatTrader fees
3. **Aircraft brokers** (light/mid jets, turboprops, helicopters) — premium transaction value, content-starved
4. **Aviation service providers** — A&P/IA, MROs, avionics — desperate for steady leads
5. **Exotic / classic auto dealers** — strong inventory but often weak digital presentation

### Mid tier
6. Mainstream independent auto dealers in the $25K–$80K used range
7. Marine surveyors and ABYC-certified mechanics
8. Specialty boat haulers + enclosed auto transport
9. Marine lenders with NMLA standing

### Lower-priority tier (only after top tier is engaged)
10. Mainstream multi-store auto franchises (corporate gatekeeping)
11. Mass-market boat retailers (already locked into BoatTrader)
12. Generic insurance brokers (better via referrals than cold outreach)

---

## How to mark the score in the CRM

In the **Lead Score** column, write a single integer 1–5. Optionally append the dominant signal in parentheses:

- `5 (premium inventory + reachable owner)`
- `4 (great fit, weak digital — phone first)`
- `3 (mid-tier dealer, worth a shot)`
- `2 (gatekept, park)`
- `1 (skip — corporate franchise)`

---

## When to re-score

Re-score a lead when:
- They reply (even negatively — informs whether they're worth nurturing)
- We see new inventory listed or social content posted
- We learn about a referral path that bypasses gatekeeping
- A demo happens and changes our read on fit
- 90 days pass with no engagement → re-evaluate or DNC

---

## The daily queue rule

The send queue each day must be composed:

- **At least 60% Score-5 leads** (today's flagship contacts)
- **No more than 25% Score-3 leads** (filler / breadth)
- **Zero Score-1 leads** (these never enter the queue)
- **Max 25 messages/day total** in Mode B controlled-send

This biases the system toward signal, not volume.
