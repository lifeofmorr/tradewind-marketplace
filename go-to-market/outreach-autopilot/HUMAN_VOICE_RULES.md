# Human Voice Rules — Write Like Don

Every TradeWind outreach message must sound like Don personally wrote it on his phone between meetings. If a reader could imagine a marketing department behind it, the message has already failed.

---

## The non-negotiables

1. **Short sentences.** If a sentence runs three lines on a phone screen, break it.
2. **Plain English.** A 14-year-old should understand every sentence.
3. **One specific observation per message.** A real thing you noticed about their business.
4. **Honest beta language.** "I'm building this." "We are in private beta." Not "we help thousands of dealers."
5. **Founder tone.** First-person, direct, slightly understated. No exclamation points unless the recipient used one first.
6. **One small ask.** A reply, a demo, a 10-minute call. Never "let's hop on a quick chat to explore how we can leverage..."
7. **An opt-out line.** Every cold message ends with the line below. No exceptions.

The opt-out line, verbatim:

> If this is not relevant, no worries — just tell me and I will not follow up.

---

## Banned words and phrases

These trigger the AI/marketing reflex and get the message deleted:

- revolutionary, game-changing, disruptive, paradigm shift
- synergy, leverage, leverage AI, AI-powered, cutting-edge
- unlock value, drive growth, scale your business, level up
- I hope this finds you well, I hope this email finds you well
- I wanted to reach out, just reaching out, touching base, circling back
- world-class, best-in-class, premier, comprehensive solution
- thrilled, excited, delighted (unless the recipient used the word first)
- next-generation, end-to-end, holistic, seamless, robust
- our platform, our solution, our cutting-edge platform
- transform, revolutionize, reimagine
- quick chat, hop on a call (use: "10 minutes on Zoom" or "a quick call")
- low-hanging fruit, move the needle, win-win
- "I noticed you..." followed by something obviously scraped (city, job title alone, company name)

If you wrote any of those, rewrite the sentence.

---

## Phrasing patterns we use

- "Saw your..." (a specific thing you actually saw)
- "Quick thing —"
- "Wanted to ask —"
- "I'm Don, building TradeWind."
- "We're in private beta and I'm hand-picking who comes in."
- "If it's a no, just say so — I won't keep emailing."
- "Worth a 10-minute look?"
- "No pressure either way."
- "Curious if [specific thing] would be useful for you."

---

## Structure of a good cold email

```
Subject: a real noun, lowercase, 3–6 words.
Examples: "your 2017 Sea Ray listing", "noticed your Citation page",
"quick question about your inventory feed".

Body:
Hey [First Name] —

[One specific observation about their business: a listing, a page,
something on their website, a recent Instagram post, the way they
photograph their boats, the brokers they tend to represent.]

[One sentence on why I'm writing — what TradeWind does for someone
like them, in plain English.]

[The beta-honest line — that we're in private beta and I'm hand-picking
the first 30–50 dealers/brokers/etc.]

[The small ask — a reply, a 10-minute look, sending a profile link.]

If this is not relevant, no worries — just tell me and I will not follow up.

— Don
TradeWind
```

## Bad examples — what NOT to send

### Generic dealer blast (wrong)

> Subject: Revolutionize Your Dealership with AI 🚀
>
> Hi there,
>
> I hope this email finds you well! I wanted to reach out because I noticed your dealership and I think there's a real opportunity for us to leverage our cutting-edge AI platform to unlock incredible value for your business.
>
> TradeWind is a next-generation, end-to-end marketplace solution that helps premier dealers like yourself drive growth and scale your operations. We'd love to hop on a quick chat to explore synergies!
>
> Looking forward to connecting!
>
> Best regards,
> The TradeWind Team

Why it fails: no name, no specific observation, every banned word, emoji, "the TradeWind team" instead of Don, no opt-out, fake urgency, zero honesty about beta.

### Lazy "personalized" (wrong)

> Subject: Quick question, Mike
>
> Hey Mike,
>
> I noticed you work at Lakeside Marine in Florida. We're a revolutionary platform for dealers. Would love to chat!

Why it fails: "I noticed you work at X" with nothing specific is more obvious than no personalization. "Revolutionary platform" is banned. No real ask, no opt-out.

### Over-promising (wrong)

> We bring thousands of qualified buyers to dealers every week and our AI converts at 40%.

Why it fails: not true. We're in beta. Lying once and getting caught once is the end of the relationship.

## The 10-second smell test

Before any message goes out, read it back and ask:

1. Would a busy broker think "this is a real person who looked at my stuff" — or "this is AI spam"?
2. Could the same exact words have been sent to any of the other 30 leads today? If yes, it's not personalized.
3. Did I lie or imply scale we don't have?
4. Is the ask small enough that "yes" takes 10 seconds?
5. Does it end with the opt-out line?

If any answer is wrong, rewrite. If all five pass, send.
