# Personalization Engine

The repeatable structure for writing a cold message that doesn't feel cold. Read `HUMAN_VOICE_RULES.md` first.

---

## The 5-part structure (use every time)

1. **Specific observation** — one real thing about their business
2. **Why TradeWind fits** — what we do for someone like them, plain English
3. **Honest beta context** — we're in private beta, hand-picking, free for 60 days
4. **Simple offer** — free profile / free listings / partner slot
5. **Low-pressure CTA** — a 10-minute look, a reply, ship a profile link

Always close with the opt-out line.

---

## Template skeleton

```
Subject: [3–6 word noun phrase, lowercase, no emoji]

Hey [First Name] —

[OBSERVATION: one specific thing — a listing, a page, an IG post, a recent change]

[WHY US: what we do for someone like them, one sentence]

[BETA HONESTY: we're in private beta, hand-picking the first N, free for 60 days, no fee until lead flow]

[OFFER + ASK: free profile / free listings / partner slot — and a 10-minute look or a reply]

If this is not relevant, no worries — just tell me and I will not follow up.

— Don
TradeWind
```

## Personalization angle library

Use the angle that fits what you observed. Each angle maps to a different reason TradeWind is interesting for them.

### Angle A — Weak listing presentation
**Trigger**: Their listings look like classified ads. Bad photos. Minimal copy. Important details missing.
**TradeWind fit**: AI-generated listing copy from their existing notes/photos, clean profile page, better merchandising.
**Example observation**:
> Saw your 2019 Freedom 285 listing — solid boat, but the gallery only has the exterior shots. Buyers usually want the helm and cabin angles first.

### Angle B — Premium inventory deserving better placement
**Trigger**: They sell genuinely high-end stuff but the surface is generic. DuPont Registry, Hagerty, BAT-quality consigners.
**TradeWind fit**: Featured placement, verified-broker badge, buyer-routing for high-intent search traffic.
**Example observation**:
> The CJ2 you have listed has the cleanest hours-since-overhaul writeup I've seen on any of the broker sites — most bury that.

### Angle C — Niche expertise that buyers actively hunt for
**Trigger**: Type-club presence, specialty (air-cooled 911s, Hinckley sportfish, classic Mooneys, SR22 maintenance).
**TradeWind fit**: We route niche buyer requests directly — they don't have to chase.
**Example observation**:
> Found your shop through the Cirrus Owners forum — the SR22 annual writeup you posted was the best I've read.

### Angle D — Active social, weak website
**Trigger**: Their Instagram is great. Their website is from 2018.
**TradeWind fit**: TradeWind profile becomes their "real" website — clean, modern, buyer-facing.
**Example observation**:
> Your Instagram is doing the heavy lifting — restoration reels are great. The website doesn't show what the IG does.

### Angle E — Could benefit from inbound leads (service / lender / insurance)
**Trigger**: Service provider with no marketing engine. Lender who relies on broker referrals only.
**TradeWind fit**: We route buyer/owner requests to the service network. Steady, qualified inbound.
**Example observation**:
> Buyers find TradeWind through search and the very next question is "who do I trust to inspect / annual / finance this." I want shops like yours on the receiving end.

### Angle F — Inventory mismatch (more listings on YachtWorld/Controller than own site)
**Trigger**: Their own website has 4 boats, but YachtWorld shows 11. They're paying a marketplace tax.
**TradeWind fit**: Profile + listings sync. Direct buyers find them on TradeWind, no per-listing fee.
**Example observation**:
> Your site shows 4 active listings, but YachtWorld has 11 of yours. You're paying to be findable somewhere else.

### Angle G — Just-launched / just-rebranded
**Trigger**: New brokerage, new website, recent rebrand.
**TradeWind fit**: Get featured during their launch window, build authority alongside us.
**Example observation**:
> Saw the rebrand — clean. New brokerages usually have a window where buyers don't know to search for them yet.

### Angle H — Top-of-market reputation that we want to validate the platform
**Trigger**: Known operator, strong reputation, others will follow them onto a platform.
**TradeWind fit**: They get founder access; we get a flagship.
**Example observation**:
> Your name comes up in every Hinckley conversation I've had with buyers — figured I'd write directly.

## How to pick the right angle

1. Open their site, IG, and one of their listings. Spend 3 minutes.
2. Ask: what's the **first thing** I'd notice if I were a buyer?
3. That's the angle. Write the observation in plain English.
4. Match it to A–H above and use the matching TradeWind fit.

If you can't find a real angle in 5 minutes, the lead is probably a 3 or lower and should not be in today's queue. Drop them and pick a stronger lead.

## Subject line patterns that work

- `your [model] listing` — refers to a specific listing
- `noticed your [page name]` — refers to a specific page
- `quick question about your inventory feed`
- `aircraft buyers looking for shops` — for service providers
- `your [city] brokerage`
- `[their specialty] buyers`

Avoid: ALL CAPS, emojis, "URGENT", "Quick chat?", "Following up", "Re:" without a real prior thread.

## CTA library

Use the lightest CTA that still moves the relationship forward. From lightest → heaviest:

1. "Worth me sending a 1-pager?" (zero commitment)
2. "Want me to ship you a profile link?" (one click)
3. "Worth a 10-minute look?" (low time)
4. "10 minutes on Zoom next week?" (one calendar slot)
5. "Want to be one of the first 5 brokers we onboard this month?" (status play, only for Score-5)

Pick **one**. Never stack CTAs.

---

## The personalization audit

Before sending, read the message back and check:

- [ ] The observation is specific enough that it could not have been sent to anyone else
- [ ] The "why us" sentence is in plain English (no banned words)
- [ ] The beta honesty is in there
- [ ] There is exactly one ask
- [ ] The opt-out line is present
- [ ] It reads like Don wrote it on his phone

If any check fails, rewrite.
