# Service Provider Beta Onboarding

You're a financing partner, insurance broker, marine surveyor, transport company, or concierge — and you're joining TradeWind's private beta. This is the fast path to receiving qualified leads.

## What "service provider" means here

Anyone who provides a service buyers and sellers need around a transaction:
- **Financing** — banks, credit unions, marine/auto lenders.
- **Insurance** — marine and auto policy brokers.
- **Inspections / surveys** — certified marine surveyors and pre-purchase auto inspectors.
- **Transport** — over-the-road haulers, marine transport, white-glove delivery.
- **Concierge** — anything else a buyer needs that doesn't fit the above (storage, detailing, registration, slip placement, etc.).

## Step 1: Sign up

Use the link we sent. It includes `?role=service_provider`. If it doesn't, pick "Service provider" on the role selector after signup.

A real business email gives you a faster admin verification.

## Step 2: Complete the onboarding form

After first login you'll land on `/onboarding/service-provider`. Tell us:
- **Business name**, address, phone, website.
- **Service categories** you cover (multi-select).
- **Service area** — states, regions, or radius from your home base.
- **Capacity** — how many leads/week you can realistically handle.
- **Pricing notes** (optional but useful) — "free quotes within 4 hours," "$650 base survey," etc. Buyers see this when matching.
- **Logo and cover image** for your public listing at `/services/your-slug`.

Admin review usually takes a few hours during beta. You'll get a confirmation email when your profile goes live.

## Step 3: Watch the leads inbox

`/service/leads` shows every request routed to you. Each lead includes:
- Buyer name and contact preference.
- The category they requested (financing, insurance, etc.).
- Listing context (year/make/model/price) when relevant.
- Any free-text notes the buyer added.

Reply via the in-app thread or take it offline if your workflow demands it — but please mark the lead status (`assigned → in_progress → completed/declined`) so admin's matching gets better over time.

## Step 4: Tune your public profile

`/service/profile` lets you edit:
- About copy. 2–3 paragraphs naming a real human and your specialties.
- Coverage map.
- Specialties and certifications (e.g., "ABYC certified," "AMS accreditation," "DOT MC #").
- Photos of your team, equipment, or sample work.

This is what shows up at `/services/your-slug` and on the matching panel admin uses.

## Step 5: What we ask of you during beta

- Respond to leads within 24 hours, even if it's just to decline.
- Tell us when a match is wrong — we're tuning the partner-match algorithm.
- Tell us when something on the buyer side felt off — e.g., a buyer asked for a survey on a unit that didn't yet have a price; that's a flow we should fix.

## What you get

- First-mover positioning on the public service-providers page.
- Direct routing of in-category leads from beta cohorts.
- Free participation through the public-launch transition.

## Things that aren't done yet

See `KNOWN_LIMITATIONS.md`. For service providers specifically:
- Lead routing is partially manual — admin reviews each request before assignment.
- We don't have a built-in invoicing tool yet. You'll bill clients through whatever channel you currently use.
- The partner-match panel suggests you to admin; admin can override.
