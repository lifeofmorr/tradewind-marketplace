# TradeWind — 10-Minute Demo Script

**Owner:** Don Morrison
**Format:** Screen share on https://tradewind-marketplace.vercel.app
**Tone:** Founder-led, conversational, premium-calm. Not a sales pitch — a product walkthrough.
**Goal:** Prospect leaves knowing 2 things TradeWind does that nothing else in their stack does, and says yes to a beta trial.

---

## Pre-Call Checklist (3 minutes before)

- [ ] Open the live site in one browser tab
- [ ] Open the prospect's website in another tab (know their inventory style)
- [ ] Pull up a listing in their vertical (boat/auto/aircraft) — pick a realistic one, not the most expensive
- [ ] Check their lead notes in `/admin/outreach` — vertical, pain point, personalization angle
- [ ] Mute notifications, close Slack/email
- [ ] Share a browser window, NOT full screen
- [ ] Have `REPLY_TO_DEMO_OPERATIONS.md` nearby for post-call follow-up
- [ ] Test your mic and camera

---

## The 10 Minutes

### 0:00–1:00 — Founder Intro (1 min)

**What to say:**
> "Hey [First name], appreciate you making time. I'm Don — I'm building TradeWind, which is an AI-powered marketplace for boats, autos, and aircraft. Rather than pitch slides, I want to show you the actual product and see if any of it's useful for how you're already doing business. Sound good?"

**Rules:**
- No slides. Ever.
- Don't describe the company mission. Show the product.
- Let them confirm before proceeding — it sets a collaborative tone.

---

### 1:00–3:00 — The Browse Experience (2 min)

**Show:** Homepage → Browse → Click into a listing in their vertical.

**What to say:**
> "Here's what a buyer sees when they land. [Click into a listing.] Full specs, photo gallery, dealer profile, pricing — all in one place. The buyer can save it, request info, or reach out to the dealer directly. No lead forms that disappear into a void."

**Personalize:** If they're a boat dealer, show a boat. If auto, show a car. Match their world.

**Key point to land:** "The buyer experience is clean and serious — no pop-ups, no bait-and-switch, no 'call for pricing.' It's designed for the buyer who's actually ready to act."

---

### 3:00–5:00 — The AI Tools (2 min)

**Show:** Pick ONE AI feature relevant to their pain point.

**Option A — Listing Generation (if they have inventory to list):**
> "Let me show you something that saves our dealers a lot of time. [Navigate to seller dashboard → New Listing.] You enter basic specs — year, make, model — and the AI writes a full listing description. Professional tone, SEO-optimized, highlights the right selling points. You review, edit, publish. What used to take 30 minutes takes 3."

**Option B — Buyer Assistant (if they get a lot of buyer inquiries):**
> "When a buyer is browsing, they can ask the AI assistant questions — 'What's the draft on this boat?' or 'Does this come with a trailer?' — and it pulls from the listing data to answer immediately. That means fewer tire-kicker calls for your team and better-qualified leads when they do reach out."

**Option C — Pricing Estimate (if they do valuations):**
> "There's a pricing estimator that pulls comparable data. It's not a replacement for your expertise, but it gives buyers a reality check before they reach out. Fewer 'what's your best price' conversations."

**Rules:**
- Only show ONE AI feature. Don't overwhelm.
- Name the pain point it solves, not the tech behind it.
- If the AI output isn't great on the demo listing, own it: "This is beta — the output quality improves as we feed it better data."

---

### 5:00–7:00 — The Dealer Dashboard (2 min)

**Show:** Dealer dashboard → Inventory tab → Leads tab → Analytics tab.

**What to say:**
> "Here's what your day looks like as a TradeWind dealer. [Show inventory.] All your listings in one place — you can feature or boost individual ones. [Show leads.] Buyer inquiries come in here with contact info, what they're looking at, and a lead score so you know who's serious. [Show analytics.] Views, inquiries, conversion — basic but it tells you what's working."

**Key point to land:** "Everything a buyer does on your listing turns into actionable data in your dashboard. No guessing about which listings are getting attention."

**If they ask about integration:** "Right now it's standalone — you manage listings here directly. We're building import tools so you can sync from your existing DMS, but that's a few months out. For beta, the fastest path is to create a few listings and see how the leads flow."

---

### 7:00–9:00 — Their Questions (2 min)

**What to say:**
> "That's the core of it. What stands out to you? Anything you'd want to dig into?"

**Rules:**
- Shut up and listen. They talk, you learn.
- Take notes on their specific reactions — these go in the daily report.
- If they ask about features that don't exist: be honest. "That's not built yet. Would that be a deal-breaker for you, or is it a nice-to-have?"
- If they ask about pricing: use the pricing script from `REPLY_TO_DEMO_OPERATIONS.md` — 60-day free beta, Starter at $149/mo after.

**Common questions and honest answers:**

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| "How many dealers are on the platform?" | "We're in private beta — you'd be one of the first. That means you get direct access to me and your feedback shapes the product." |
| "Where do the buyers come from?" | "Right now, organic search and direct traffic. We're building SEO landing pages for specific markets. Early stage — I won't pretend we have a million monthly visitors." |
| "What makes this different from [competitor]?" | "The AI tools and the focus on serious buyers. But honestly, the best way to compare is to try it with a few of your actual listings and see if the leads are better quality." |
| "Is my data safe?" | "Yes — hosted on Supabase (AWS infrastructure), row-level security on every table, SSL everywhere. Your listing data belongs to you and you can export or delete at any time." |

---

### 9:00–10:00 — The Ask (1 min)

**What to say:**
> "[First name], here's what I'd suggest — I can set you up with a beta account today, you list 3–5 of your current inventory, and we see what happens over the next couple weeks. No cost, no commitment. If it's not useful, you walk away with nothing lost. If it is, we talk about what a long-term partnership looks like. Want to give it a shot?"

**If yes:**
> "Great — I'll send you a beta invite link right after this call with a quick setup checklist. Should take about 15 minutes to get your first listings up. I'll check in with you in a few days to see how it's going."

**If maybe/not now:**
> "Totally fair. I'll send you a one-pager you can share with your team, and I'll check back in a week or two. No pressure."

**If no:**
> "Appreciate you taking the time to look. If anything changes, you know where to find me."

## Post-Call Actions (within 1 hour)

1. **Log the call** in the daily report — outcome, key reactions, objections, next step
2. **Send follow-up email** within 2 hours:
   - If yes: beta invite link + onboarding checklist
   - If maybe: one-pager PDF + "let me know if any questions come up"
   - If no: brief thank-you, no attachment
3. **Update pipeline** — move from "Demo scheduled" to appropriate stage
4. **Note any feature requests** — these inform the roadmap

---

## What NOT To Do

- Don't demo for longer than 10 minutes unless they're asking questions
- Don't show the admin panel (internal only)
- Don't show features that aren't built yet as if they're live
- Don't claim traction you don't have — "first dealers" is fine, "100 dealers" is not
- Don't badmouth competitors — "different approach" is fine, "they're terrible" is not
- Don't demo on your phone — always desktop screen share
- Don't skip the ask — if you showed the product, you earned the right to ask
- Don't send the demo recording without asking — some people don't want to be recorded
